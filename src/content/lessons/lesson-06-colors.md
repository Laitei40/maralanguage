Adjectives in Mara typically follow the noun they describe.

## Colors (Colour)

| Color | Mara |
| :--- | :--- |
| White | **Nâ** |
| Black | **Vaw** |
| Red | **Sâ** |
| Green | **Hnaw** |
| Yellow | **Vavâ** |
| Blue | **Dypa** |

## Common Adjectives

| English | Mara |
| :--- | :--- |
| Good | **Pha** |
| Bad | **Pha-lei** / **Vaw** (evil/bad) |
| Big | **Lai** |
| Small | **Chy** |
| Hot | **Sâ** (also Red/Heat) |
| Cold | **Vaw** (context depends) / **Chaka** |
| Beautiful | **Ngia** / **Pha** |

## Sentence Structure

**Noun + Adjective**

*   **Pâ** (Flower) + **Sâ** (Red) -> **Pâ sâ** (Red flower)
*   **O** (House) + **Lai** (Big) -> **O lai** (Big house)

**Noun + Subject Marker + Adjective**

*   **He pâ he a sâ.** (This flower is red.)
*   **Ei o a lai.** (My house is big.)

> **Note:** "A" often functions as a subject marker or part of the verb phrase.

## Practice

1. Point to a white object and say: "**He he a nâ.**"
2. Describe your shirt color.
3. Say "Good morning" (Morning Good) -> "**Mydi pha**".
