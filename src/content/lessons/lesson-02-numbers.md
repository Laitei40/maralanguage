Counting is essential for everyday life. In Mara, numbers are straightforward. This lesson covers the basic cardinal numbers (1-10).

## The Numbers 1-10

| Number | Mara | Pronunciation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | **Kha** | kha |
| 2 | **Nô** | noh |
| 3 | **Thoh** | thoh (aspirated t) |
| 4 | **Pali** | pa-li |
| 5 | **Pangaw** | pa-ngaw |
| 6 | **Charu** | cha-ru |
| 7 | **Sari** | sa-ri |
| 8 | **Charie** | cha-rie |
| 9 | **Chaki** | cha-ki |
| 10 | **Hraw** | hraw |

## Tone and Rhythm

When counting in sequence, keep the rhythm steady.
*   **Nô** and **Thoh** are short and sharp.
*   **Pali** and **Pangaw** have stress on the second syllable.

## Usage Examples

In Mara, the number usually follows the noun it is counting.

*   Vui **kha** (One bear)
*   O **nô** (Two houses)
*   Chapaw **thoh** (Three boys)

## Practice

1. Count from 1 to 10 aloud.
2. Count backwards from 10 to 1.
3. Try to say your phone number partially in Mara digits.
